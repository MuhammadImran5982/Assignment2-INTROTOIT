Job description and What made the job appealing to Louisa

The role requires me to manage senior leaders whilst bringing technological “thought” to the team thus, combining business with technology. The core of this role is the embedding of Information Technology business-wide, whilst also aiming to remove the blockades that can come from a lack thereof. Further, project management would mean taking charge of a team, striving them to bring out the best in their abilities to ensure goals and deadlines are met. I was drawn to this role because I believe I was a born leader. The job attracts good pay, and working over a team are positive benefits, however it requires experience from past jobs, and I will not be able to obtain the job overnight. It requires good networking, soft skills, communication and reliability to name a few things. All of these skills I believe I have the basic fundamentals of, however I would love to broaden my skills in all of these areas. Hence, having an end goal which is not strivable straight away excites me and keeps me on track throughout my work and educational career thus far.

It can be very rewarding to help organisations defend against cyber-attacks and know that you are making a difference.

Groups required skill set (General and IT related)

For our team, we have a relatively even split amongst group members that want to end up working more with the technical aspects of IT as well as working more in the management side of IT. The three members who want to work more in the management side of things are Louisa, Lachie, and Muhammad who want to work as a CIO, Project lead and cyber security analyst respectively. The other two members of our group, James and Marcus, wish to work in more technical aspects with James wanting to work as a software engineer and Marcus wishing to work as a front-end developer. As the IT field is continuously growing, demand for all the jobs that our group members seek to work as increases with every year.

Across the three members who are looking to work in more managerial roles, they all have a clear demand for good communication, which is ranked as the highest in demand skill as of March 2018, as well as a good understanding of the technical elements that they will be working around. Due to all three of these members being responsible for working within and managing teams, their communication skills in addition to the organisation and team management skills must be extremely good, with the ability to clearly relay information to members of their team being pivotal in their role. Additionally, as both Lachie and Muhammad would be working directly with technical aspects of their work their understanding of the necessary components of the technology must be extremely good as to understand the work that is being completed in their teams, in addition to contributing to the technical aspects of their work themselves. While Louisa is required to also have deep knowledge of technologies, her role requires to more broadly know about new technologies that can be implemented into the business that she is working in to further make the goals of the company be reached.

As for the two members who wish to work with the technical aspects of IT, the primary skills that would be required of them would be their deep understanding of the technical tools that they are working with, whether that be a specific programming language they are working with or deep knowledge of other related tools that will help them within their role. As for James wanting to become a software developer, the demand for software engineering skills ranked as 15th out of 25 in March 2018, however the rank of software engineer was 11th out of 25 in the top job titles at the same time. Similarly for Marcus, graphic design skills ranked 10th out of 25, which would be a vital skill in being a front-end developer. While graphic design skills would help Marcus, the need for web developers ranked as 15th for job titles. Both James and Marcus ultimately would need deep understanding of their specific skill sets as to remain competitive within their respective fields
